About Me

Hi! My name is Grace. I’m 19 years old and currently studying Criminology at the University of the Cordilleras. Since I was young, I’ve always been curious about how laws work and how people can help keep their community safe. That’s why I chose this path — to one day serve and protect with kindness and strength.

I enjoy meeting people, learning new things, and facing challenges that make me grow. I try to stay positive, even during hard times. I believe that we become better every time we try our best and never give up. I also love spending time with family and friends, because they are my support system in life.

I’m simple, friendly, and always willing to learn. I know I still have a lot to experience, but I’m excited for what’s ahead!

My Achievements

I feel proud of what I have achieved so far, even if there’s still a long way to go. In elementary, I became a classroom officer and SSG leader. I was awarded Athlete of the Year in Grade 6 and even got to compete in Palarong Pambansa, which was an amazing experience.

In high school, I received honors for doing well in my studies. I also joined a cooking contest in our town and won! It made me more confident in myself. Now in college, I’m part of our department’s sports team and still aiming to keep my grades high while doing what I love.

Every award and achievement means a lot to me, because it shows that hard work really pays off.

My Schools

My education started at Tetep-an Elementary School in Sagada. I learned the basics and made good memories with my classmates and teachers. For junior high school, I went to Antadao National High School, where I gained more confidence and got better at handling responsibilities.

In senior high, I transferred to Sagada National High School and took the HUMSS strand. It helped me become more interested in people and how society works. Now, I’m studying Criminology at the University of the Cordilleras. I feel thankful for each school that helped me grow, learn, and believe in my dreams.
